## Project Summary
An interactive Power BI dashboard delivering dynamic visuals, filters, and a robust data model built using ETL processes, Power Query, Power Pivot, and DAX. The solution unifies insights across functions and markets to improve visibility and support strategic decisions. AtliQ Hardware sells computers and accessories through three main channels:
- Retailers  
- Direct Sales  
- Distributors  
The company faced losses due to a poorly planned store expansion in the USA, driven by basic survey analysis and Excel-based decisions. This highlighted the need for a **data-driven analytics solution**.

This project delivers an **interactive Power BI dashboard** that provides a comprehensive **4-year business performance analysis** across key departments:
- Finance  
- Sales  
- Marketing  
- Supply Chain  
- Executive Overview  

Users can analyze data by quarter, year-to-date, and year-to-go, comparing against last year or targets. The dashboard is highly interactive, with bookmarks, clear abbreviations, and brand-aligned design.

From January to August 2022, the dashboard reflects estimates based on forecasts, and the final months align with AtliQ’s financial calendar—especially focusing on peak festive-season sales (such as Diwali promotions). The goal is to act now so that these improvements positively impact the year-end peak season and beyond.

## Dashboard Views

- **Home Page** – Central landing page organizing navigation and an Info & Support section (bookmarks).
- **Finance View** – P&L statements and financial KPIs across markets, products, customers, and fiscal periods.
- **Sales View** – Customer performance, net sales trends, product benchmarking, and market comparisons.
- **Marketing View** – Segment and campaign performance across regions and years.
- **Supply Chain View** – KPIs such as forecast accuracy, net error, and product movement.
- **Executive View** – High-level KPIs, revenue contributions, and trend summaries for leadership.

## Insights:

### Finance Insights:
Net sales in 2021 grew over 200%, yet net profit was negative (-6.63%). High operational costs and steep discounts led to margin erosion despite strong sales.

### Sales Insights:
Amazon, AtliQ Exclusive, and AtliQ eStore were top customers, while smaller accounts (e.g., Nova, All-Out) dragged overall performance. Notebooks led product revenue, while networking and storage segments underperformed.

### Marketing Insights:
Regionally, the EU showed a positive net profit (1.4%), while APAC and NA were loss-making. Market share grew slowly, but competitors like Dell remained dominant.

### Supply Chain Insights:
Forecast accuracy reached 80%, but net error remained high. Many customers faced either excess inventory or stockouts.

### Executive Insights:
PC division drove nearly half the revenue, and retail channels contributed 70% of sales. Direct and distributor channels were underutilized.

### Overall:
In 2021, AtliQ Hardware’s market share improved, even though it is still at the bottom compared to competitors. Dell, followed by Innovo, has the highest market share.

## Recommendations by Dashboard:

### Finance:
- Focus on cost control, especially operating expenses.  
- Improve pricing discipline to reduce discounting and protect gross margins.  
- Ensure that sales growth translates into sustainable net profit growth.  

### Sales:
- Diversify your customer base to reduce reliance on top clients like Amazon.  
- Target underperforming customers with improvement plans or consider phasing them out.  
- Leverage top-selling products like notebooks while addressing underperforming categories.  

### Marketing:
- Focus marketing efforts on profitable segments and regions, like the EU.  
- Build brand strength in areas where AtliQ is gaining market share.  
- Develop campaigns to boost lagging product lines like storage or networking.  

### Supply Chain:
- Do SKU-level forecasting instead of only overall forecasting  
- Reduce net error through continuous forecast adjustments  
- Align inventory with profitability, not just demand  
- Prioritize high-demand products and reduce excess stock of slow-moving items  
- Prepare inventory planning for festive demand spikes (Sep–Oct)  

### Executive:
- Diversify revenue sources by expanding underutilized channels (direct, distributor).  
- Continue building market share while focusing on profitability, not just top-line growth.  
- Shift from aggressive growth toward balanced, sustainable scaling that improves net profit.  
- Diversify beyond the PC division  

---

## Impact:
- By acting on these recommendations, AtliQ can improve its profitability by the 2022 festive season and end the year on stronger financial situation.
- They can turn around underperforming regions or products and enter 2023 with a more balanced growth trajectory.  
- Increased market share through better strategic and data-driven decisions.
